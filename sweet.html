Pearl Millet Halwa

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup pearl millet flour (bajra flour)
  • 1/2 cup ghee
  • 1 cup jaggery or sugar
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • Chopped nuts for garnishing (almonds, cashews, pistachios)

steps to cook:

  1. Heat ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan.
  2. pearl millet flour (bajra flour) and roast it on low heat until it turns aromatic and changes color slightly. This step is crucial to remove the raw taste of the flour
  3. In a separate pan, heat water and jaggery (or sugar) until the jaggery melts and forms a syrup. Strain the syrup to remove impurities.
  4. Slowly add the jaggery syrup to the roasted bajra flour while continuously stirring to avoid lumps.
  5. Keep stirring the mixture on low heat until it thickens and starts to leave the sides of the pan
  6. Add cardamom powder and mix well
  7. In another small pan, heat a little ghee and roast the chopped nuts until they turn golden brown.
  8. Add the roasted nuts to the halwa mixture and mix well
  9. Cook for a few more minutes until the halwa reaches the desired consistency
  10. Turn off the heat and let the halwa rest for a few minutes.
  11. Serve the warm Pearl Millet Halwa garnished with more nuts on top

Foxtail Millet Ladoo

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup foxtail millet flour
  • 1/2 cup jaggery or sugar
  • 2-3 tablespoons ghee
  • Cardamom powder
  • Chopped nuts (optional)

steps to cook:

  1. Heat ghee in a pan and roast foxtail millet flour until it turns aromatic and light brown.
  2. Add jaggery or sugar to the roasted flour and mix well until the jaggery melts and combines evenly
  3. Add cardamom powder and chopped nuts for extra flavor
  4. Allow the mixture to cool slightly so you can handle it.
  5. Shape the mixture into small round balls (ladoos) while it is still warm
  6. Your delicious Foxtail Millet Ladoos are ready to be enjoyed!

Jowar Fruit Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked jowar grains
  • Assorted fruits (like apples, bananas, oranges, berries)
  • Lemon juice
  • Honey or sugar (optional)
  • Mint leaves for garnish

steps to cook:

  1. Cook the jowar grains according to package instructions and let them cool.
  2. Chop the assorted fruits into bite-sized pieces and place them in a mixing bowl
  3. Add the cooked jowar grains to the fruits.
  4. Squeeze some fresh lemon juice over the salad for a tangy flavor.
  5. If you prefer it sweeter, you can drizzle some honey or sprinkle a bit of sugar.
  6. Mix everything gently to combine all the flavors
  7. Garnish with fresh mint leaves for a pop of color and extra freshness.
  8. Your Jowar Fruit Salad is ready to be served!

Ragi Burfi

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup finger millet flour (ragi flour)
  • 1 cup grated jaggery
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 cup ghee
  • Chopped nuts (like almonds, cashews)
  • Cardamom powder

steps to cook:

  1. Heat a pan and dry roast 1 cup of finger millet flour (ragi flour) on low heat until it turns aromatic. Keep stirring to prevent burning.
  2. In a separate pan, add 1 cup of grated jaggery and 1/2 cup of water. Heat the mixture until the jaggery melts and forms a syrup. Strain to remove impurities if necessary.
  3. Add the roasted finger millet flour to the jaggery syrup and mix well to avoid lumps.
  4. Continue cooking the mixture on low heat while stirring continuously.
  5. Add 1/4 cup of ghee and keep stirring until the mixture thickens and starts leaving the sides of the pan.
  6. Mix in chopped nuts (almonds, cashews) and a pinch of cardamom powder for extra flavor.
  7. Grease a plate or tray with ghee and transfer the mixture onto it. Spread and flatten the mixture evenly.
  8. Allow the mixture to cool down and set for some time.
  9. Once set, cut the mixture into desired shapes like squares or diamonds.
  10. Your delicious Ragi Burfi is ready to be enjoyed! Store in an airtight container.

Little millet kheer

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup little millet
  • 4 cups milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • Saffron strands
  • Chopped nuts (like almonds, cashews, pistachios)

steps to cook:

  1. Rinse 1/2 cup of little millet thoroughly and soak it in water for about 30 minutes.
  2. In a heavy-bottomed pan, bring 4 cups of milk to a boil
  3. Drain the soaked little millet and add it to the boiling milk. Cook on low heat, stirring occasionally.
  4. Allow the little millet to cook in the milk until it becomes soft and the mixture thickens, this may take around 20-25 minutes.
  5. Add 1/2 cup of sugar (adjust to taste) and mix well until the sugar dissolves completely.
  6. Add a pinch of saffron strands and 1/4 tsp of cardamom powder for flavor.
  7. Continue to cook the kheer on low heat until it reaches a desired consistency.
  8. In a separate pan, dry roast chopped nuts (almonds, cashews, pistachios) until they turn golden
  9. Add the roasted nuts to the kheer and mix well.
  10. Turn off the heat and allow the kheer to cool slightly before serving.

Koda millet rice

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup kodo millet
  • 1 cup jaggery (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 cup grated coconut
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • 2 cups water
  • Ghee (clarified butter)
  • Ghee (clarified butter)

steps to cook:

  1. Rinse 1 cup of kodo millet thoroughly and soak it in water for about 30 minutes.
  2. In a pan, add 2 cups of water and bring it to a boil.
  3. Drain the soaked kodo millet and add it to the boiling water. Cook until the millet is soft and water is absorbed.
  4. In a separate pan, melt jaggery with a little water to make a syrup.
  5. Once the kodo millet is cooked, add the jaggery syrup and mix well.
  6. Add 1/2 cup of grated coconut and 1/4 tsp of cardamom powder to the mixture and stir.
  7. In another pan, heat some ghee and roast chopped nuts (cashews, almonds) until golden.
  8. Add the roasted nuts to the sweet rice mixture and mix gently.
  9. Cook for a few more minutes until everything is well combined and the flavors meld together.
  10. Turn off the heat and let it sit for a few minutes before serving.
