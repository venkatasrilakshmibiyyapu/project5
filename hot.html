Pearl Pulao

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup pearl millet (bajra)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 green chili, chopped
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups water
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil

steps to cook:

  1. Rinse the pearl millet thoroughly and soak it in water for about 30 minutes.
  2. In a pressure cooker or a deep pan, heat oil and add cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  3. Add chopped onions, green chili, and sauté until onions turn golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Cook until tomatoes are soft.
  6. Drain the soaked pearl millet and add it to the pan. Mix well with the masala.
  7. Add water, stir, and close the lid. Pressure cook for 3-4 whistles or cook covered until the pearl millet is cooked and water is absorbed.
  8. Once done, fluff the pulao gently with a fork.
  9. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

Foxtail Upma

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup foxtail millet
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 green chili, chopped
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • A few curry leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil

steps to cook:

  1. Dry roast the foxtail millet in a pan until it turns slightly golden and gives off a nutty aroma. Set it aside.
  2. In a separate pan, heat oil and add mustard seeds. Let them splutter.
  3. Add cumin seeds and curry leaves, followed by chopped onions. Sauté until onions turn translucent.
  4. Add chopped green chili, grated ginger, and sauté for a minute.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook until tomatoes are soft.
  6. Add the roasted foxtail millet and mix well with the masala.
  7. Pour in the water, stir, and cover the pan. Let it cook on low heat until the millet is cooked and water is absorbed.
  8. Fluff up the upma with a fork and serve hot.

Jowar Khichidi

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup jowar millet
  • 1/2 cup yellow moong dal (split yellow mung beans)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

steps to cook:

  1. Rinse the jowar millet and moong dal. Soak them in water for about 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a pressure cooker or pot, heat ghee and add cumin seeds.
  3. Add chopped onions, garlic, and ginger. Saute until onions turn golden brown.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften.
  5. Add turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well.
  6. Add the soaked jowar millet and moong dal. Stir to combine with the spices.
  7. Pour in water and give it a good stir.
  8. Close the pressure cooker lid and cook for about 3-4 whistles or simmer in a pot until the millet and dal are cooked through.
  9. Once done, garnish with fresh coriander leaves before serving

Ragi Roti

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup ragi flour (finger millet flour)
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped onions
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped tomatoes
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp chopped coriander leaves
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Oil or ghee for cooking

steps to cook:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine ragi flour, chopped onions, tomatoes, green chili, coriander leaves, cumin seeds, turmeric powder, and salt
  2. Mix all the ingredients well.
  3. Slowly add water little by little to make a soft dough. The dough should be pliable and not too stiff.
  4. Divide the dough into equal-sized balls.
  5. Take a ball of dough and place it on a greased sheet of parchment paper or a banana leaf.
  6. Flatten the dough ball with your fingers to form a round roti.
  7. Heat a tawa or skillet over medium heat.
  8. Carefully transfer the flattened roti onto the hot tawa.
  9. Cook the roti on both sides until it is cooked through and has golden brown spots.
  10. Drizzle some oil or ghee around the edges of the roti while cooking.
  11. Remove the roti from the tawa and serve hot with chutney, pickle, or yogurt.

Little Potato Cutlet

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked and mashed potatoes
  • 1/2 cup cooked little millet
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped onions
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped bell peppers
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped
  • 1/2 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • 1/2 tsp coriander powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Bread crumbs for coating
  • Oil for frying

steps to cook:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine mashed potatoes, cooked little millet, chopped onions, bell peppers, green chili, ginger-garlic paste, garam masala, cumin powder, coriander powder, and salt
  2. Mix all the ingredients well to form a uniform mixture.
  3. Take a small portion of the mixture and shape it into a cutlet.
  4. Roll the cutlet in bread crumbs to coat it evenly.
  5. Heat oil in a pan for frying.
  6. Once the oil is hot, carefully place the cutlets in the pan.
  7. Fry the cutlets until they are golden brown and crispy on both sides.
  8. Remove the cutlets from the pan and place them on a paper towel to absorb excess oil.
  9. Serve the delicious Little Millet Potato Cutlets hot with ketchup or chutney.

Koda Masala Rice

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup kodo millet
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, finely chopped
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped
  • 1/2 inch ginger, grated
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • Oil for cooking

steps to cook:

  1. Rinse 1 cup of kodo millet thoroughly and soak it in water for about 30 minutes.
  2. In a pan, add 2 cups of water and bring it to a boil.
  3. Drain the soaked kodo millet and add it to the boiling water. Cook until the millet is soft and water is absorbed.
  4. In a separate pan, melt jaggery with a little water to make a syrup.
  5. Once the kodo millet is cooked, add the jaggery syrup and mix well.
  6. Add 1/2 cup of grated coconut and 1/4 tsp of cardamom powder to the mixture and stir.
  7. In another pan, heat some ghee and roast chopped nuts (cashews, almonds) until golden.
  8. Add the roasted nuts to the sweet rice mixture and mix gently.
  9. Cook for a few more minutes until everything is well combined and the flavors meld together.
  10. Turn off the heat and let it sit for a few minutes before serving.
